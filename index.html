Health Effects of Khat

This website provides information about the health effects of khat, a plant native to East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. Khat leaves are commonly chewed for their stimulating effects. Please note that the information provided here is not from a doctor. If you require more information or have specific concerns, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional.

What is Khat?

Khat (Catha edulis) is a plant that contains a natural stimulant called cathinone. The fresh leaves of khat are chewed. The active compounds in khat can produce stimulating effects similar to amphetamines.

Reasons Why People Chew Khat

  • Social and cultural reasons: Khat has a long history of traditional use in certain cultures, where it is often chewed during social gatherings or used as a symbol of hospitality.
  • Stimulation and alertness: Khat contains stimulant compounds that can produce feelings of alertness, and euphoria.
  • Mood enhancement: Some individuals chew khat to experience a temporary boost in mood and a sense of well-being.
  • Increased sociability: Khat is believed to enhance sociability and conversation, making it popular in social settings.
